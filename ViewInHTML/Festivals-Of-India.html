Festivals of India - S.Bagchi Combined
Name of States or, UTs Name of Festivals Key Points
Ladakh Hemis Festival
  • It is celebrated in the month of June.
  • It is celebrated in the Hemis Monastery of Ladakh.
  • It is celebrated to mark the birth anniversary of Guru Padmasambhava.
  • It is celebrated by the Buddhists.
Jammu & Kashmir Tulip Festival
  • Srinagar hosts the great Tulip Festival every year
  • Indira Gandhi Memorial Tulip Garden in Kashmir is Asia's largest tulips garden.
Shikara Festival
  • Kashmir's government began this festival in 2016.
  • It is celebrated in the Dal Lake of Srinagar.
  • Shikaras participate in Shikara Race, Dragon Boat Race and Canoe Polo Match.
Himachal Pradesh Kullu Dussehra
  • It is celebrated in the month of October.
  • It is celebrated in the Kullu Valley of Himachal Pradesh.
  • It is celebrated to mark the victory of Lord Rama over Ravana.
  • It is celebrated by the Hindus.
Minjar Festival
  • It is celebrated in the month of July.
  • It is celebrated in the Chamba Valley of Himachal Pradesh.
  • It is celebrated to mark the blooming of Maize and Paddy.
  • It is celebrated by the Hindus.
Halda Festival
  • It is celebrated in the month of December.
  • It is celebrated in the Lahaul Valley of Himachal Pradesh.
  • It is celebrated to mark the blooming of Maize and Paddy.
  • It is celebrated by the Hindus.
Punjab Baisakhi
  • It is celebrated in the month of April.
  • It is celebrated in the Punjab.
  • It is celebrated to mark the harvest of Rabi crops.
  • It is celebrated by the Sikhs.
Lohri
  • It is celebrated in the month of January.
  • It is celebrated in the Punjab.
  • It is celebrated to mark the harvest of Rabi crops.
  • It is celebrated by the Sikhs.
Haryana Surajkund Mela
  • It is celebrated in the month of February.
  • It is celebrated in the Surajkund of Haryana.
  • It is celebrated to mark the harvest of Rabi crops.
  • It is celebrated by the Hindus.
